# Site Goals and Target Audience

## Site Goals

All design choices must be justified against client goals and audience. The rubric explicitly penalises "design without justification" — visual and structural decisions should trace back to one of the goals below.

### Primary Goals

1. **Drive enrolments** — get visitors to sign up for Hot Shots, Squad Training, or Private Classes. Every page should lead toward a CTA.
2. **Promote court hire** — 10 courts available, 24/7 booking via play.tennis.com.au. The CTA strip on the homepage directly serves this.
3. **Build trust in the coaching program** — showcase coaches, the Kalynda Chase Regional Tennis Centre location, and the program philosophy ("BELIEVE - LEARN - ACHIEVE - SUCCEED").
4. **Inform about programs** — clearly explain the four program types (Hot Shots, Squad, Private, Tournament Travel) and the Social Tennis fixtures so visitors know what's available and relevant to them.

### Secondary Goals

- Provide a contact point for Tim Robinson and the centre
- Surface the social tennis schedule for existing members

---

## Target Audience

| Segment | Who | What they need |
|---|---|---|
| **Parents of young children** | Primary — Hot Shots is a kids program. Parents in the Kalynda Chase / North Queensland area looking for structured activities | Program details, enrolment CTA, trust signals (coaches, photos) |
| **Recreational adult players** | Squad Training, Social Tennis, court hire | Session times, how to book, court availability |
| **Competitive/aspiring players** | Private Classes, Tournament Travel | Coaching credentials, performance pathway |
| **Existing members** | People already enrolled | Fixtures schedule, court booking, contact info |

**Geographic scope:** North Queensland — Kalynda Chase area. The site serves a specific local community, not a national audience. Design should feel approachable and community-oriented.

---

## Design Implications

These link design decisions back to goals and audience — required for exemplary marks under the Goal-Driven and Interface Design criteria.

- **High-contrast, energetic colour palette** — tennis is an active sport; reflects energy without being overwhelming for parents browsing on mobile
- **CTAs on every page** — the rubric scores "calls-to-action are well used" at exemplary level
- **Mobile-first** — parents searching for kids activities primarily browse on phones
- **Coach photos and names** — key trust signal for parents choosing a program for their child
- **Clear program hierarchy** — Hot Shots → Squad → Private → Tournament reflects a natural player progression; information architecture should mirror this pathway

The client tagline **"BELIEVE - LEARN - ACHIEVE - SUCCEED"** is the brand anchor — it signals character development alongside skill, speaking directly to the parent audience.
